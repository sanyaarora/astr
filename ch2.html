Nonparametrics (Chapter 5)

This part of the course described nonparametric approaches to statistics, which are useful when trying to estimate the distribution of a dataset without any prior knowledge about the data. We studied many different tests that can be used to assess the distribution of data. Some of these tests are:

Kolmogorov-Smirnov Test: Used to compare the distribution of a sample against a hypothesized distribution (one-sided) or to compare the distributions of two independent samples (two-sided). For example, it can be used to assess whether the distribution of star brightness in two different galaxies follows the same distribution.

Mann-Whitney U Test (or the Wilcoxon rank-sum test): A nonparametric test that is used to compare two independent samples to determine whether their population distributions are shifted from each other. Unlike the KS-test, it assess shifts in the center of the distributions, rather than analyzing the shape of the distributions.

Wilcoxon Signed-Rank Test: A test for comparing two paired samples to determine whether their population mean ranks differ. It is designed to compare two related samples, or repeated measurements on a single sample. It is also useful for analyzing data in situations where the population cannot be assumed to be normally distributed.

Spearman's Rank Correlation Coefficient: used to measure the strength of the correlation between two variables. It can be used to determine either positive or negative correlations, and the p-value is used to determine the statistical significance of the correlation.

Nonparametric techniques have very broad applications to most scenarios when one cannot make assumptions about the underlying distribution of data. A specific example of when it could be used would be evaluating the impact of an environmental factor on the health of different regions without assuming a specific distribution for the environmental factors.
